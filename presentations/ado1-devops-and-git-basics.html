DevOps

Origin

Silos caused frustration among industry leaders

  • Slow turnaround times
  • Failures & quality
  • Unnecessary complexity and paperwork

Agile & lean concepts applied to SDLC (specifically Deploy and Build)

Definition(-ish)

Practices that combine traditionally Dev and Ops responsibilities into one mindest with cross functional teams and a focus on collaboration, communication, and flow

No formal definition

Key point: DevOps is tools and culture

Principles (CAMS)

    Culture

    Automation

    Measurement

    Sharing

Culture

Not just a tools discussion

Shifting mentality to think agile and flow (especially leadership)

Automation

Tools facilitate SDLC process and flow

Help streamline, identify risks, give feedback, reduce human error

Measurement

Make sure our processes are becoming better

How does app performance change when implementing DevOps?

MTTR and MTTF

Sharing

Dev and Ops work as a unified team - no invisible wall

Share WIP, responsibilities, app's lifecycle, etc

Transparency is key

The Three Ways

Increase flow

Enhance Feedback Loops

Encourage experimentation

Increase Flow (Shift Left)

Work should always flow downstream

Test early and often

Don't ever pass a known defect downstream

Identify and reduce bottlenecks and rework

Enhance Feedback Loops

Create multiple short feedback loops (Dev -> Ops, Business -> DevOps, etc)

Improvement, analysis, retrospectives

Automated testing gives developers feedback

Encourage Experimentation

Learn from success and failure

Encourage teams to take intelligent risks
